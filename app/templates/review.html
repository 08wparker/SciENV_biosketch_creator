C Products

Provide a list of: (i) up to five products closely related to the proposed project; and (ii) up to five other significant products that highlight the senior/key person's Contributions to Science. The NIH Biographical Sketch Supplement will provide the opportunity to describe these contributions in more depth while referencing the other significant products cited in this section.

Acceptable products must be citable and accessible including by Persistent Identifier (PID), and may include:

  • publications, conference papers, and presentations;
  • technologies or techniques;
  • patents, copyrights, and licenses;
  • inventions, germplasm, and other collections;
  • data, databases, and other materials; and
  • other products such as data, databases, or datasets, physical collections, audio or video products, software models, educational aids or curricula, instruments, or equipment, research materials, interventions (e.g., educational or behavioral), or new business activities.

Each product must follow the NIH Policy on Use of Hypertext in NIH Grant Applications and include full citation information.

C Contributions to Science (0/5 selected)

All senior/key persons should complete the "Contributions to Science" section. Briefly describe up to five of your most significant contributions to science. While all applicants may describe up to five contributions, graduate students and postdoctorates may wish to consider highlighting two or three they consider most significant.

For each contribution, include:

  • the historical background that frames the scientific problem;
  • the central finding(s);
  • the influence of the finding(s) on the progress of science or the application of those finding(s) to health or technology; and
  • your specific role.

Figures, tables, or graphics are not allowed.
